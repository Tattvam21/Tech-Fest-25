The Evolution of Computers

First Generation (1940-1956)

The first computers used vacuum tubes for processing and were extremely large. The ENIAC was one of the earliest examples.

Second Generation (1956-1963)

Transistors replaced vacuum tubes, making computers smaller, faster, and more reliable. IBM 1401 was a popular model.

Third Generation (1964-1971)

Integrated circuits (ICs) were introduced, reducing size and increasing efficiency. The IBM System/360 was a key development.

Fourth Generation (1971-Present)

Microprocessors were introduced, making personal computers (PCs) widely available. Apple and IBM PCs became common.

Fifth Generation (Present & Beyond)

Modern computers use AI, quantum computing, and cloud technology. Devices like smartphones and supercomputers define this era.
